Working Smart With What You Have

Power & data

  • Edit in batches when you have power — export overnight if you're on a generator schedule.
  • Download stock sounds/assets once, reuse offline — don't rely on data for every project.
  • Export at the resolution the platform needs, not higher — saves time and data on delivery.

Gear that's enough

  • A mid-range phone with a stable CapCut workflow beats a laptop you can't afford yet.
  • Good lighting and steady footage matter more than an expensive camera.
  • Reinvest your first earnings into one upgrade at a time — not everything at once.

Getting paid safely

  • Use mobile money for local clients — agree the amount in writing (even just WhatsApp text) first.
  • For remote/foreign clients, Payoneer or a trusted contact's bank account works when direct transfer isn't possible.
  • Never send the final, watermark-free file before the full payment lands.

Protect Your Time

South Sudan's market is small — your reputation travels fast. These habits protect it.

"Just do it for free, I'll pay you later" — later rarely comes. Agree a price before you start, always.
"Just for exposure" — exposure doesn't pay data bills. It's fine only if you're choosing it deliberately for a portfolio piece.
Endless revisions with no extra pay — agree upfront how many rounds of edits are included.
Sending the final file before payment — always deliver a watermarked preview first.
